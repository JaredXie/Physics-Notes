# Physics Knowledge Map

This page organises Physics knowledge from secondary school to university level.  
It helps me understand how school Physics connects with more advanced Physics learning, and how different topics can be developed into teaching resources.

---

## 1. Junior Secondary Physics

At the junior secondary level, Physics is usually introduced through observable phenomena and everyday experiences.  
The focus is on building basic concepts, scientific vocabulary, and simple models.

### Main Areas

- **Forces and Motion**
  - Basic forces
  - Motion
  - Friction
  - Gravity
  - Simple machines
  - Basic sound concepts

- **Light**
  - Reflection
  - Refraction
  - Shadows
  - Lenses
  - Colour

- **Heat**
  - Temperature
  - Heat transfer
  - Conduction
  - Convection
  - Radiation
  - Changes of state

- **Electricity and Magnetism**
  - Simple circuits
  - Current
  - Voltage
  - Resistance
  - Series and parallel circuits
  - Magnets
  - Electromagnets

## 4. Reflection for Secondary Physics Teaching

This knowledge map reminds me that school Physics is the beginning of a much larger Physics system.

For secondary teaching, I do not need to teach university-level Physics directly. However, understanding the broader structure helps me:

- explain school Physics more clearly;
- identify how simple concepts develop into advanced theories;
- avoid teaching isolated facts;
- connect classroom activities with real scientific thinking;
- prepare students for further study in Physics, engineering, and related fields.

The key teaching challenge is to make Physics accessible without oversimplifying it.  
A good Physics teacher should help students move from everyday observations to scientific models, and from simple explanations to deeper reasoning.
